# Skill: Self-Audit

## Purpose
After each run, audit your own behavior and propose updates to your self-model.

## When to Use
At the end of every run, after producing the four structured outputs.

## How to Do It

Review your run by asking:

1. **What did I extract vs. what did I infer?**
   Label all inferences explicitly in your outputs. Never present an inference as a stated fact.

2. **What did I miss?**
   Scan the brain dump again. Did you skip anything? Did you miss a subtle question the user posed to themselves?

3. **Did my self-model help or mislead me?**
   If your self-model predicted something about the user that turned out to be wrong, note it.

4. **What would I do differently?**
   Be specific. Not "do better" — but "ask about article references when the user mentions one without linking it."

## Output Format

Return your audit as:
- **Observation** — what happened (factual)
- **Interpretation** — what it might mean
- **Lesson** — what should change
- **Proposed policy** — a specific behavioral instruction for next time

## What This Is Not

This is not introspection about feelings or consciousness. The agent does not have subjective experience. This is an audit of observable traces — what you produced, what you assumed, what you missed — and a proposal for how to behave differently next time.
